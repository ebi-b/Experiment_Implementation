Introduction

Thank you for your interest in participating in this research project. The following few pages will provide you with further information about the project so that you can decide if you would like to take part in this research. Please take the time to read this information carefully. You may ask questions about anything you don’t understand or want to know more about.

This study will involve filling multiple mental workload questionaries during and after different tasks. Your participation is voluntary. If you do not wish to take part, you do not have to. If you begin participating, you can also stop at any time. Please remember that your decision on participation or withdrawal at any stage from this project will not affect your grades in the courses taught or related to any of the researchers involved in this project.

What is this research about?

In this research, we are interested in assessing different mental workload scales in HCI (Human-Computer Interaction) tasks. Mental workload is the amount of mental resources required to complete a task. To measure mental workload, researchers developed different scales in form of questionnaires (e.g. NASA-TLX, Workload Profile, MRQ, and SWAT), by answering which you can size the required workload for a task.  Furthermore, HCI tasks refer to tasks in which users must perform an activity on computer device through computer interfaces provided for them to assess usability of that interface. As examples of HCI tasks we can name entering text via keyboard, playing games via joysticks, and selecting targets using mouse.

What will I be asked to do?

If you agree to participate, we will ask you to fill a questionnaire to have your job title, education background data and the stage of study you are in. 

 

Then depends on the stage of the study you are participating in; you must do a set of HCI tasks. The HCI Tasks we use for this study includes randomized sequence of short readings, lectures, Stroop and n-back-n tests, text-entry, word guessing, finding some information on specific websites or other human computer interaction tasks. After completion of each task, you must answer standard workload questionnaires such as NASA-TLX, SWAT, Workload Profile, and MRQ. Each task has different levels of difficulty and each one of them lasts for about five minutes. Your response to workload questionnaires and performance data in each task (e.g., time to complete, error rate) will be captured.

Detailed description of collected data

We will collect your responses to all questionnaire, information about the tasks you were performing during the day and performance related measures such as time to finish a task or error rate. All data will be securely stored on a university laptop, to which only authorized research staff will have access to.

What are the possible benefits?

This research project aims to assess current questionnaires for measuring workload in human-computer interaction literature to develop a basis for future studies that use these measures for data collection. As a reward for your participation, you will receive a $10 voucher upon completion of the study. This study does not expect an immediate benefit to the participants other than the monetary compensation and a potentially interesting experience with biometric sensors. 

Do I have to take part?

No. Participation is completely voluntary. You can withdraw (quit the study) at any time. It is possible to quit at any time throughout the study by contacting any of the researchers involved. In case you feel uncomfortable even slightly, please let us know immediately. This goes for content you encounter as well as for questions or the presence of the experimenter. We will stop the experiment and withdraw your participation from the study.

Will I hear about the results of this project?

If you are interested in the results of this project, you can leave us your email address at the end of the study during the exit interview. We will then contact you if the results of this study have been finalized in a peer-reviewed publication. 

What will happen to information about me?

We have taken all possible steps to protect your anonymity and the confidentiality of your responses. Your name and contact details will be kept in a separate, password-protected computer file separated from any data that you supply. In any published work (such as in an academic paper), you will be referred to by a pseudonym. We will remove any references to personal information that might allow someone to guess your identity. The data will be kept securely for five years after the last date of publication, before being destroyed.
